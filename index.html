01 — Front end

Before the visit: can you see this patient, and for how much?

Verification of benefits, prior auth, and patient responsibility shouldn't be a 40-minute phone call per patient. They shouldn't be a Google Sheet. And they definitely shouldn't be the reason a patient gets told one number at intake and billed another after the visit.

02 — Reconciliation

After the visit: did the money actually land?

The ERA says 120. The bank shows 119. Six weeks later a recoupment nibbles it back. Your biller books it off a spreadsheet from the RCM director. Nobody has the full picture — until they do.

  • Full ERA parsing from the clearinghouse — denials, partial denials, recoupments, contractual adjustments, patient responsibility, payer fees — across payer formats.
  • ERA-to-bank matching via Plaid. When the ERA says 120 and 119 hits, we name the Zealous dollar.
  • Denials with reasons — CARC / RARC classification, resolution tracking, biller notes preserved.
  • Point-in-time claims picture — not "what the EMR shows," but what the payer and the bank agree on right now.
Built for MSO structure from day one

One parent entity. Sub-practices underneath. Clinicians under those.
Payout splits and audit trails all the way down.

Most reconciliation tools assume one entity, one bank account, one billing pipeline. That works for one practice. It breaks at three. It's unrecoverable at ten. Our data model is built around nested entities — so as you add divisions, states, or specialties, the pipeline extends instead of shatters.

◆

Nested entity model

MSO → sub-practice → clinician, with payout splits, tax IDs, and ownership percentages encoded — not jammed into a spreadsheet tab.

◇

EMR-agnostic

Works with Prompt, InSync/Sora, WebPT, and others. We pull from the clearinghouse and the bank, not from your biller's posting conventions.

◈

Every payment rail

Direct ACH, payer ERAs, mailed checks, mobile deposits, patient copays. If money can reach you, we can reconcile it.

◇

Multi-specialty ready

OT, PT, SLP under one roof — with discipline-specific modifier logic, payer rules, and denial patterns learned across the whole network.
